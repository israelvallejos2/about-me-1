About Me

  • I was born and raised in the Los Angeles area with a close-knit family. I remember as a kid looking at one of the magazines I'd get from the Scholastic book form that had a program written in it. I would sit in front of the family Apple IIc and wonder what the heck the program was doing. For some reason, I never asked my older brother if he could explain it to me. It was, however, the first code I ever looked at.
  • I have two bachelor's degrees. The first is in English from University of California - Riverside (UCR). The second is from California State University, Northridge in Manufacturing Systems Engineering. I like to joke that I have two BS degrees.
  • During each degree, I was exposed to several different languages through classes: Fortran 77, C++, and Assembly Language. I really enjoyed learning Assembly. At UCR even had a computer in my dorm room that had Linux installed that my friend ran a "talker" on it. My friend helped me program a game of tic-tac-toe in C for the talker. I also had a job working in the computer labs had been exposed to Macs, Windows, Linux, and Unix machines by that point. (Remember Usenet?)
  • Jobs that I have held have included teaching 8th grade English and manufacturing engineer. I have now spent the last 15 years in manufacturing. First I worked for Raytheon, now I work for Boeing. Both of these jobs have included programming of some kind. At Raytheon, I worked on robots that used a variant of C or C++. Currently, I am working on automating time consuming, but repititious tasks my team does using a combination of VBA and Python.
  • My goal is to eventually take the education from Code Fellows and turn it into a full-time job. I enjoy puzzles of all sorts: Jigsaw, cryptic crosswords, escape rooms, and more. (I've even created a few puzzles for a MicroSoft event called Puzzle Safari.) Programming is very much like a puzzle to be solved and will bring me more fulfillment than my current work tasks that don't involve programming.
